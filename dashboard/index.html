About Landru

Landru is an advanced repository housing the codebase for creating and training a machine learning model that utilizes traffic simulation data for large-scale traffic optimization. Our project leverages reinforcement learning techniques on virtual traffic networks to understand traffic patterns, predict traffic conditions, and optimize traffic flow.

Empowering cities with AI-driven traffic management solutions.

Minimizing traffic jams and improving overall traffic flow.

Eco-Friendly

Decreasing emissions and promoting sustainable urban mobility.

Our Goals

Understanding Traffic Patterns

Analyze simulated traffic flow to gain insights into vehicle behavior, congestion dynamics, and potential bottlenecks.

Predicting Traffic Conditions

Develop a trained model capable of forecasting future traffic states, allowing for proactive management and mitigation of congestion.

Optimizing Traffic Flow

Utilize the model's insights to design and evaluate strategies for optimizing traffic signal timing, route planning, and infrastructure improvements.

Our Approach

1

Data Collection

Utilize OpenStreetMap (OSM) data as the primary source. This involves accessing road networks, traffic signals, and other pertinent details from OSM. The extracted data is processed to convert it into UXSIM-compatible traffic data.

2

Model Training

Train a machine learning model using reinforcement learning techniques to understand and predict traffic patterns.

3

Evaluation

Assess the model's performance in predicting traffic conditions and optimizing traffic flow through simulation-based experiments.

4

Deployment

Integrate the trained model into real-world traffic management systems for practical applications.
